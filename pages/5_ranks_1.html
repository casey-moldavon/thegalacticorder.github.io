RANK CHANGE INFO

Promotions, after Rank 1, may occur when a member has either achieved the necessary prerequisites for the desired promotion or by choice of a superior Officer of Rank 3 or higher. Officers may also promote others to a rank below their own.

Warning: Promotions can be prevented from occurring within a battalion if there is limited availability for the desired role. In these instances, transfer requests may be made to leadership for re-assignment to a different Battalion where the position is available. If no existing battalion can offer the role, the individual will be put on a promotion wait list, placement of which is determined by promotion offer date. While awaiting promotion availability, a unit may continue to progress with further prerequisites, which may set them apart from other listed candidates, or they may request a Division Change.

This is the process of changing / transferring from one Division to another. When this occurs, regardless of a member’s current rank, they must begin at the lowest level of said division unless acquired prerequisites qualify for a higher rank or they are returning to a division role previously held. With Division Returns, members are typically offered the rank they last held in that division unless otherwise stated by a member of Leadership (Rank 4 or higher).

To start this process, a unit must contact their superior officer privately to request a transfer. The Officer will then consult their superior and the current acting Commander of the desired Division before an approval can be given. Once Approved, the change is made.

For most positions, there is a list of available achievements that may be completed in order to qualify for that particular role/rank. These are listed on each Rank Information Unit card, under Prerequisites. This list often includes a variety of missions, tasks and experiences related to the desired role, reflecting the type of responsibilities that position will entail. This typically falls under 4 primary categories, each with multiple sub-categories:

Rank Service:

This is split into 2 sub-categories: Hours & Months, referring to consistent time spend as the current rank.

  1. Hours: Number of hours spent in-game as current rank.
  2. Months: Number of months since last promoted to current rank.

Note: These are specific to the current rank only and get reset after each promotion or division change. This is not only to avoid consistent division changes but to also prevent rapid promotion rates.

Order Service:

Currently this represents the number of months serving within The Galactic Order. This prerequisite category is mostly used for promotions into Rank 3 or Leadership.

Dedication:

This is split into 3 sub-categories: # of Events, # of Recruits & Patrols.

  1. # of Events: Number of Galactic Order Events participated in since joining.
  2. # of Recruits: Number of members recruited to join the Galactic Order since joining.
  3. Patrols: Number of Roleplay Patrols reported for duty since joining.

Note: These apply to most promotion prerequisite lists, if not all, in some way. They also do not reset like Rank Service prerequisites or have restrictions similar to Missions Completed prerequisites.

Missions Completed:

This is split based on division along with the missions, tasks and responsibilities that each division has, paired with the requirements of the desired role. On the roster, each mission type is color-coded and includes a brief description. These will also be listed below:

    Mission Types:
  • Turret: Any operation of a manned or remote turret. (combat division)
  • Ground Assault: Any 1st person mission that involves discharging a weapon. (combat division)
  • Escort: Accompanying or escorting a superior officer as a protective detail. (combat & medical division)
  • Inquiry: Investigation related missions requiring criminal analytics & enforcement of Law & Order. (combat division)
  • Pilot / Co-Pilot: Operating on a ship terminal as co-pilot, operating a remote turret or flying a ship personally. (engineer division)
  • Mining: Operating a mining laser to any degree (in a vehicle or on foot). (engineer division)
  • Salvage: Performing Ship repairs and utilizing a salvaging tool or ship of any kind. (engineer division)
  • Cargo: Any transportation of goods, resources or objects from one location to another (regardless of profit). (engineer division)
  • Transport: Any transportation of people, targets or individuals in need of aid. (engineer & medical division)
  • Support: Resupplying a ship, lending aid, providing quality of life assistance, providing loans, etc… (engineer & medical division)
  • Search & Rescue: Recovering someone from a destroyed or disabled ship, recovering a body, healing someone, healing one-self or providing healthcare of any kind. (medical division)

    • Note: Science Division not yet available...

  • Manage Data: Transportation or manipulation of data in any way. (science division)
  • Research: Exploration, scanning or conducting tests for the purpose of furthering technological knowledge. (science division)
  • Agronomy: Any Agricultural practices or gathering of biological resources / samples. (science division)

Promotion Exams are optional assessments that ranked Galactic Order members may take in order to progress through the ranks at a more accelerated rate. These are encouraged for individuals whom are passionate about moving up and understand the responsibilities a higher rank would entail.

Different Exams apply to various ranks and divisions, listed under Prerequisites for each Unit Card in Rank Information. Current available Exams are listed below with a brief description.

Note: Exams are commonly administered by Commanders & 1st Officer of an Academy Battalion.

  • Protocol Certification Exam: This applies to all division positions Rank 1 - 3. It tests Roleplay protocols and basic gameplay rules, all of which can be found here on The Galactic Order Info site.
  • G-Tactical Exam: This tests the applicant's Ground combat knowledge, referring to FPS combat gameplay. This assessment applies to the Shock Trooper & Commander roles of the Combat Division.
  • S-Tactical Exam: This tests the applicant's Ship/Space combat knowledge, mostly with a focus on a Trooper's role and not aspects related to piloting and/or engineering. This assessment applies to the Lieutenant & Commander roles of the Combat Division.
  • Officer Exam: This tests the applicant's knowledge related to aspects of command, incorporating scenarios and testing the individual's overall Galactic Order knowledge. This assessment applies to the Commander roles of each Division
  • Medical Tier 2 Exam: This tests an applicant's medical knowledge, with a focus on tier 2 Medical gameplay. This assessment applies to the Lieutenant & Commander roles of the Medical Division.

Warning: Each exam may only be applied to once per week and Applicants may only fail twice before restricted from further attempts indefinitely.

Note: Any Member may apply to the Exams listed above regardless of Rank or Division.

Note: Additional details are given for each Exam when applying via private message to an Officer Rank 3 or higher.

RANK UNKNOWN: CONSCRIPT

Acting much like a contracted mercenary, Conscripts operate outside the official ranks of The Galactic Order military. They have no restrictions to the rules and regulations with regards to attire, responsibilities and command. However, this also means they hold no authority in the chain of command and receive no benefits or unit allowances. Conscripts are often hired under a general contract with very specific parameters based on a percentage of funds earned upon completion of assisted missions and tasks.

RANK 0: NOVITIATE

Novitiate is the starting Rank within The Galactic Order military. However, they have a very unique role with nearly full access to the tasks and opportunities available to rank 1 units, with exceptions to Equipment, Authority and Benefits. A Novitiate may work as a Trooper, Operator or Medic (within reason) to determine their preferred future branch. Upon their 1st promotion to Rank 1, a Novitiate may choose between the 3 available Divisions (Combat, Engineering or Medical). Although a division choices can be changed, it is viewed as a long-term commitment of duty. For this reason, it is recommended that each Novitiate study the roles and responsibilities of each option before making a decision.

    RANK 3: AGENT

    Agent is an alternate progression option within the Special Ops sub-division, achievable through promotion from Outrider (only). Because Outrider progression is not recorded officially, they will not be privileged to details regarding their promotion prerequisites and will simply be notified when they qualify.

    Special Note: The Promotion to Agent will require a comprehensive and advanced trial, testing the individual’s skills, knowledge and understanding of what the role would entail. This trial may be given at any time without warning, but only to Outriders whom have shown an expressed interest in the promotion and have completed the minimum promotion prerequisites.

    Agents are high-priority covert units of the Special Operations sub-division branch of the Combat Division. They operate completely off-grid, existing purely within the shadows, unregistered to battalions or rosters and with their identities known only to the highest ranked members of the Leadership division.

    They specialize in deep-cover espionage, infiltration and assassination operations, often on assignment for long periods of time to eliminate political leaders and destabilize government bodies. They’ve also been rumored for use to eliminate corrupt members within the Galactic Order itself. In many instances, they will be planted within divisions as a different rank, either under orders or to simply occupy their down-time between assignments.     RANK 4: CAPTAIN

    More commonly found aboard larger ships and vessels, Captains are held to the highest of standards, with nearly complete authority and command over their own personal battalion which may consist of multiple ships, up to 25 crew and specific roles, all of which the captain claims full responsibility.
