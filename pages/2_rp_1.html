PROTOCOL

With any roleplay game/setting, each person holds the responsibility for playing a character on some level. Of course, this can simply be a reflection of the player themselves but enjoyment can also be found in developing something new. A character unlike one-self that can change and develop over time is always a rewarding experience and brings a lot of unique dynamic elements of gameplay into effect. This can take any form such as a simple bullet point list of traits, a short paragraph of likes & dislikes or a written backstory…or maybe all 3 if you feel ambitious. The only limitations for any of these are based on the established lore of the world or, in this case, the universe of Star Citizen and The Galactic Order.

It is worth noting that creation of a personal character lore is not mandatory by any means and can be made or edited at any time, at the creator’s discretion. This experience is greatly encouraged though, as it can add a lot of fun and emersion to the overall experience within the org. It is also important to note that there are no expectations on performance or portrayal of one’s character. Expert-level acting is appreciated but absolutely not required or expected.

As stated in our Rules & Laws, each unit is responsible for wearing the armor and assigned uniform for their position. Unit specific attire is an important part of the Galactic Order’s image, acting not only as a projection of power but also further developing the chain of command, giving visible differences between ranks.

Noticeably, lower ranks will be presented with more armor, weapons and tools to signify the risks they take and the challenges they face in harsher work enviornments. As the ranks increase in number, units will typically lose armor, personal storage, weapons and tools as they become further burdened by the challenges of command. The only exception to this rule is with the main branch of the combat division, which sees an increase in armor leading up to the position of officer. Although Commanders may don the equipment and uniform of any same-division rank 1 or 2 for the purpose of combat, it is typically less common as they hold a level of responsibility that shouldn’t be risked unless absolutely necessary.

There is no limit or specific regulation with regards to the weapons each unit may equip at a given time. However, some units have Tool requirements that may prevent them from equipping weapons.

Medical Division units must always have quick access to drug pens and a ParaMed Medical Device equipped for instant use.

Engineer Division units must always have a Multitool equipped for instant use and quick access to any of its attachments.

This is the only instance when regulation equipment, such as Armor & Tools, are not enforced as there will be times when items may need to be worn or replaced for the sake of performing the task of item retrieval. However, after looting is complete, all regulations are immediately set back in place.

  1. Use Salute emote when first addressed by an Officer.
  2. Use Salute emote when dismissed by an Officer.
    • Officers will typically Salute back but are not required to.
  3. Use Attention emote after Salute until Officer says "at ease".
  4. Use At Ease emote when Officer says "at ease".
    • Units may resume duties only after Officer says "dismissed" or "as you were".
  5. Use Attention emote when an Officer enters the room.
    • First unit to see Officer calls personnel in the room to Attention emote by saying: "Officer on Deck!".
    • Units may resume duties only after Officer says "dismissed" or "as you were".
  6. An Officer is any unit Rank 3 or higher.
  7. Unit Conduct is temporarily suspended during Combat or similar critical settings

Note: Officers must also follow Unit Conduct with Suprirors of higher rank.

Note: Officers sharing the same rank use Salute emote when first addressing each-other, but no other Emotes or actions are needed after.

Note: These codes do not need to be followed in Habitation areas for Sleeping, Eating, Rest and Relaxation.

As part of a regular Patrol, units must report for active duty to their Commander or superior Officer while following Unit Conduct.

  1. Use Salute emote when first addressing an Officer.
  2. State Rank and Name followed by "Reporting for Duty".
    • Example: "Ensign Cr4zy reporting for Duty".
  3. Use Attention emote after Salute until Officer says "at ease".
  4. Use At Ease emote when Officer says "at ease".

Note: Units may resume duties only after Officer says "dismissed" or "as you were".

Note: Officers saying "dismissed" or "as you were", in this context, indicates they have begun to log progression.

Note: Patrols end when unit either signs off or "Requests Relief". Requesting a relief is when a unit requests to be off-duty. This follows identical protocol as Reporting for Duty.

Important Note: Officers are considerd on Patrol (On-Duty) any time they Report to a Superior Officer, an Officer of the same rank or any time a unit reports for duty to them.

When off-duty, units who are following proper equipment protocol may independently report any completed Hours and Missions to their Commander.

  • Combat Division units report to Trooper-Commander
  • Engineer Division units report to Engineer-Commander
  • Medical Division units report to Medic-Commander

If there is no acting division commander within a unit’s Battalion, they may report to another Commander or the acting 1st Officer.

Leadership Division, indluding 1st Officers, holds responsibility for recording # of Events and # of Recruits for each Unit.

An act of insubordination is a defiance towards authority and a refusal to obey orders.

For every act of insubordination, an individual will be issued a private message and a warning. As stated in our Rules & Laws, members will often receive 1 Warning before facing demotion or harsher punishments.

For Novitiates, "harsh punishment" will most often mean termination. The Galactic Order will not tolerate disobedience and will not dedicate time & resources towards the progression of an individual whom has shown no interest in remaining among our ranks or improving the organization.

Note: Some failures in protocol may count as an act of insubordination after repeated offenses…

Uniform Warnings / Alerts:

Every time a unit must be reminded to wear the proper uniform, they are issued a Red A alert next to their name on the roster. When 3 alerts have been issued, this will count as an act of insubordination. These may be issued by any officer and will result in a private message and 1st warning before further action is taken.

Warnings Removal:

Some alerts may be removed from the roster sheet at leadership's discretion. This typically occurs on unit allowance days or after some time has passed. Official Warnings will occasionally take longer to be removed.
